Chilaquile Dish

Description

Chilaquiles is a traditional Mexican breakfast or brunch dish featuring crispy corn tortilla chips simmered in a savory green or red salsa until they begin to soften. It is typically served immediately to preserve some of the chips' crunch before they absorb too much sauce.

Core Ingredients

  • Tortillas: Either fresh corn tortillas, which are cut into triangles and fried, or store-bought tortilla chips.
  • Salsa: A red (salsa roja) or green (salsa verde) sauce serves as the base for the chilaquiles.
  • Eggs: Fried eggs are a common topping.

Salsa Ingredients (for a homemade sauce)

  • Dried Chiles: Ancho or guajillo chiles for salsa roja.
  • Tomatillos: For salsa verde.
  • Onion and Garlic: Essential for flavor
  • Tomatoes: Add depth to the sauce.
  • Cilantro: Provides a fresh, herbaceous note.
  • Spices: Cumin and Mexican oregano are often used.

Toppings & Garnishes

    • Cheese: Cotija or queso fresco are traditional choices.
    • Avocado: Sliced avocado adds creaminess.
    • Cilantro: Fresh, chopped cilantro for garnish.
    • Onion: Finely diced white or red onion.
    • Crema: Mexican crema or sour cream for a drizzle.
    • Radishes: Thinly sliced radishes add a crisp, peppery element.
    • Lime: A squeeze of fresh lime juice brightens the dish.

Steps

  1. Fry Tortillas:
    Corn tortillas are cut into wedges and fried until crispy, or store-bought tortilla chips can be used.

  2. Prepare Salsa:
    A red or green salsa is made from ingredients like tomatoes, tomatillos, onions, garlic, and dried chiles.

  3. Toss Tortillas:
    The fried tortillas are gently tossed in the warm salsa.

  4. Add Toppings:
    The dish is topped with cheese, crema, and eggs, then garnished with onion, cilantro, and avocado.
