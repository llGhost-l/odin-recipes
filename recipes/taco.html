Taco Dish

Description

This recipe describes how to make classic ground beef tacos with a flavorful, seasoned filling and fresh toppings. It's a simple, customizable meal that is perfect for a weeknight dinner.

Core Ingredients

  • Mexican Chorizo: The main ingredient, often sold in ground or link form. If it's in links, remove the casing before cooking.
  • Tortillas: Typically corn tortillas, though flour can also be used.
  • White or Red Onion: Finely diced for a sharp, fresh contrast to the rich chorizo.
  • Cilantro: Freshly chopped for a bright, herbaceous flavor.
  • Lime: Sliced into wedges for a final squeeze of acidity.

Common Toppings

    • Onion
    • Avocado
    • Queso Fresco
    • Lime Wedges
    • Salsa

Steps

  • Cook the Chorizo: Heat a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chorizo and cook, using a spatula to break it apart into smaller pieces.
  • Add Aromatics: Once the chorizo is mostly browned, add the diced white onion and poblano pepper to the skillet.
  • Finish Cooking: Continue to cook the chorizo and vegetables until the chorizo is fully cooked and browned, and the onions are softened, about 8-10 minutes total.
  • Warm the Tortillas: While the chorizo cooks, heat the tortillas in a separate dry skillet or on a gas burner until pliable and lightly charred.
  • Serve: Top with diced onion, cilantro, and serve with lime wedges and other desired toppings.
