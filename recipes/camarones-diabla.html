Camarones A La Diabla Dish

Description

Camarones a la diabla are a classic Mexican dish of shrimp simmered in a spicy, smoky, and flavorful red chile and tomato sauce, often with a hint of sweetness and garlic.

Ingredients

  • Dried chiles: I use a combination of guajillo and de arbol chilies and pasilla, ancho, and/or New Mexico for added complexity.
  • Crushed tomatoes: Canned crushed tomatoes are fine here.
  • Onion: You can use white or yellow onion.
  • Garlic: Fresh garlic is key here. Don't substitute garlic powder or granulated garlic.
  • Salt: Don't be tempted to leave out the salt as it is essential for providing depth and balance.
  • Olive oil: Use a nice, flavorful olive oil if you have one.
  • Shrimp: I prefer medium or large shrimp.

Steps

  1. Stem and deseed the chiles then cover them with boiling water.
  2. Remove the chiles from the water and put them in a blender or food processor with the tomatoes, onion, garlic, and salt and process until smooth, adding a bit of water or the reserved chile soaking water as needed.
  3. Sear the shrimp in a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  4. Add the sauce to the shrimp in the skillet, toss to coat, and then simmer until the sauce is bubbling and thickens a bit.
